AI Ideas List

A running list of places where I think AI could make everyday life meaningfully easier, safer, or more human. Updated as I think of them.

Health & Wellness

  • 🩺AI that reads your lab results and explains them in plain language — what's flagged, what it means, what to ask your doctor.
  • 💊Medication interaction checker that runs in real time as prescriptions are added, flagging conflicts before they reach the patient.
  • 🧠Mental health check-ins embedded into daily apps (calendar, notes) that detect stress patterns and suggest micro-interventions.

Education

  • 📚Personalized tutors that adapt explanations to how each student actually learns — not just difficulty level, but style and pace.
  • ✍️Writing feedback tools that teach revision skills rather than just correcting, so students learn why a change improves the work.
  • 🌐Real-time translation of lectures and materials for non-native speakers, preserving tone and technical accuracy.

Safety & Accessibility

  • 🦯AI navigation for visually impaired users that describes environments conversationally, not just turn-by-turn.
  • 🚨Community safety tools that identify hazardous road conditions or infrastructure issues from crowd-sourced photos before accidents happen.
  • 🏠Smart home systems that learn the routines of elderly individuals and alert family if something seems off — without being invasive.

Everyday Life

  • 🛒Grocery planning that accounts for what's already in your fridge, your dietary goals, and what's on sale — and generates a meal plan around it.
  • 📬Email triage that learns your priorities over time and surfaces what actually needs your attention versus what can wait.
  • 🌱Carbon footprint tracking embedded in purchase flows — so you see the impact of choices at the moment you're making them.

Civic & Government

  • 🗳️Plain-language summaries of ballot measures and legislation, generated from official text — no spin, just clarity.
  • 🏛️AI that helps people navigate bureaucratic processes (benefits applications, permits, appeals) that are currently gatekept by complexity.
  • 📊Public budget visualizers that translate city or state spending data into something citizens can actually understand and engage with.
