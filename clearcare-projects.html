The Project

Goals & Problem Space

Young adults navigating healthcare independently often receive clinical and lab reports filled with jargon they can't decipher — and no accessible way to make sense of them. ClearCare addresses this gap by generating AI-powered, plain-language summaries of uploaded medical documents, paired with interactive visualizations and multilingual support.

"A lot of healthcare was not designed with the user care aspect in mind. ClearCare was built to extend that care beyond the physical space."
Reflection

Process, Learnings & Contributions

Scope

The scope of our MVP was to develop a working prototype that helps patients ages 18–26 better understand medical terminology in their clinical and laboratory reports. We identified several core features essential for addressing this challenge: generating an AI-powered summary of uploaded medical reports, providing plain-language explanations for complex medical terms, offering hover or click-to-define functionality for terminology, and supporting multilingual translation. We also prioritized visualizations to help users interpret health indicators and source-level indicators to ensure explanations are supported by reliable medical references.

Functionality

  • AI Summary: After scanning the uploaded report, the system generates a summary highlighting the most important information so users don’t need to read the entire document themselves.
  • Plain-Language Explanations: Medical terms are broken down into simple, easy-to-understand language accessible to anyone regardless of health literacy level.
  • Multilingual Translation: Users can translate explanations into their preferred language, improving accessibility for international users.
  • Data Visualization: The system generates graphs to display health indicators, helping users quickly understand results rather than parsing complex numbers or medical terms.
  • Reliability: All explanations are supported by trusted sources such as peer-reviewed research and clinical guidelines, with clickable references for deeper reading.

Usability

We emphasized accessibility features — multilingual translation support and simplified language — throughout the design process. Recognizing that our primary stakeholders include non-English speakers and individuals with low health literacy, we designed ClearCare to be approachable and easy to navigate without prior technical or medical knowledge. A lot of healthcare was not designed with the user care aspect in mind; ClearCare extends that care beyond the physical space.

Validation

We entered user validation with three assumptions: that users would trust the information presented, find the features accessible, and see ClearCare as essential to their care. Feedback confirmed users appreciated the simplified explanations, but surfaced concerns around HIPAA compliance and design inconsistencies. In response, we standardized range graphs across the results page, added citation links to generated content, and unified design choices across the product to build a more trustworthy and consistent user journey.

My Individual Contributions

  • Leading product strategy and execution as PM for a team building an AI powered tool which translates complex medical reports into plain language summaries.
  • Researched AI ethics and policy around usage in the intersection of medical field and technology especially with HIPPA.
  • Utilized Miro as platform to define product vision, scoped MVP features including AI explanations, multilingual translation, and color-blind accessibility support

Key Takeaways

  • Trust is built through consistency. Design and content inconsistencies were the biggest trust-breakers in user testing — especially in a health context.
  • Compliance can't be an afterthought. HIPAA concerns surfaced early and reinforced that privacy architecture needs to be foundational from day one.
  • Accessibility is a spectrum. Designing for low health literacy and non-English speakers pushed us to question every default UX assumption we had.
